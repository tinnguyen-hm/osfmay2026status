The month in one read

A quiet month, lifted by one onsite and returning members.

Three things to take away before the numbers.

Volume settled, as expected.
171 orders in May, down from the Q1 surge. May falls in the Q2 rest between the Q1 and Q3 campaigns we set at the annual review. The month tracked to plan.
One onsite drove most of the month.
The May 26 onsite at Saint Francis produced 52 scans in one day against a 3-per-day baseline. The final week around the event accounted for nearly half of May's orders.
Returning members are a growing share.
48 of the 170 members who ordered in May (28%) also ordered in 2025. Members come back when the first pair works, which is the outcome the program is built to produce.

For discussion

Two things we'd like OSF's input on.

Lifting survey engagement

The annual survey returned 69 responses from 8,232 sent. That reads overall sentiment but is too thin to segment by site, role, or tenure. Segmented outcomes are what strengthen the Q4 ROI review.

Open question for OSF
  • Would in-flow prompts at delivery, onsites, and the 6- and 12-month marks work better than a standalone survey?
  • Is a small participation incentive acceptable within OSF guidelines?
  • What response rate would OSF consider credible for outcomes reporting?
Summer onsite plan

The summer onsite plan is drafted and ranked. A few decisions need OSF to move.

Open question for OSF
  • The summer plan starts at SFMC Peoria (July 7–9) and runs five hospital waves through August. Can OSF confirm site contacts and internal comms windows for each wave?
  • Each site has a Patient Care Services lead intro as a key ask. Can OSF facilitate those introductions ahead of Wave 1?
  • Should SFMC get a second event week if July 7–9 traffic warrants it?
  • We want to prompt for survey responses at onsites. Is there a preferred format, and is a small incentive acceptable within OSF guidelines?
